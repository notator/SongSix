About Song Six: Evolving Ideas

Act Two of my Retrospective was completed in June 2024 by the writing of Retrospective 2024.
The items presented in Song Six: Evolving Ideas are therefore being created as part of the Retrospective's final scene, Song Six.
In Song Six, I am trying to find out what I really believe, in a wider context beyond the world of music and music notation. In other words, I want to develop a philosophy that brings all my experience together. This is a process that will end only when I become too weak or die.
The Evolving Ideas list can include any relevant material, including: 
  • original work (essays etc,)
  • commentaries on public material (e.g. YouTube videos) by other creators.
  • interviews with other creators (text or video)
  • the edited results of interactions that take place in an associated Patreon channel.
    Feedback and cooperation are essential in Song Six, so I have created a new bridge to the outside world in the form of an associated Patreon channel (June 2026).
    This Patreon is free to use, and is designed to be a node in a network of Patreon channels and other media. It uses a dedicated mailbox (evolvingideas@james-ingram.de) to promote interaction.

Song Six

We are at the trial of Orestes at the Temple of Apollo. It is just before dawn. Time has stopped.
Apollo and all the Muses are present. Orestes, the Sun-God, is hiding. His mother, Clytæmnestra, Goddess of the night, is dead. The Furies are asleep, but dreaming and growling.
Clytæmnestra's ghost appears. She berates the Furies, urging them to wake up and chase Orestes across the sky. Time must start up again. A new day must begin!
