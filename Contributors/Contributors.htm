James Ingram

Curriculum Vitae 		I've always been interested in the philosophy of time.
Read lots of philosophy, especially in my 20s and 30s (Russel, Popper etc.). Discovered quite late that I've always been an idealist, on encountering Hoffman (2016) and Kastrup (2022).
I think contemporary philosophy needs an interdisciplinary component.
It needs input from both professional philosophers (historians of philosophy) and those with direct experience of the world in other areas.
My own experience leads me to make a sharp distinction between perceived time (as in music) and clock time (as in physics).
I Became Karlheinz Stockhausen's copyist (1974-2000) because I wanted to grapple with the 20th century music notation crisis at close quarters.
Ended up in a practical job, rather than in academia, because I've always been sceptical of the institutional structures that have grown up around the Arts and Sciences.
Began programming in 1982. After 2000, I have been programming open source applications in C# and Javascript, and maintaining a website.
