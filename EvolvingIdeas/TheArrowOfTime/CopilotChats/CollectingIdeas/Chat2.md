# **Proposed Logical Order**

## **1. Framing & Method**

1. Introduction

   * Provisional-knowledge attitude; science as evolving models (Kuhn quote reference).
   * Motivation: reconciling Arts and Sciences.

## **2. Core Thesis**

2. The Two Types of Time

   * Define **physical (reversible)** time.
   * Define **perceived (irreversible)** time.
   * Why distinguishing them is necessary for unifying Arts and Sciences.
   * Heraclitus / Flux as philosophical background.

## **3. Wolfram’s Contribution**

3. Wolfram’s Idea of Time as State Change

   * Time defined by state transitions.
   * Computational irreducibility and unpredictability.
   * Where this aligns with the two-time framework.
   * Where it falls short (missing the Art/perception dimension).

4. Three Examples of “ticks”

   * (a) Einstein’s physical clock
   * (b) Wolfram’s cellular automaton
   * (c) Neuron firing
   * Highlight: physical regularity vs computational synchronization vs biological irregularity.

## **4. Functions, Computation, and Time**

5. Functions and state

   * Functions as input → output with optional internal state.
   * Define “tick” as one execution.
   * Physical clocks as reversible functions.
   * Neurons as irreversible functions.

6. Why irreversibility matters

   * You cannot reconstruct inputs from a neuron’s output.
   * This asymmetry *creates* perceived time.
   * Entropy becomes an *observed* phenomenon rather than a physical one.

7. Types for inputs & outputs

   * Physical clocks: booleans → booleans.
   * Neurons: numbers → events.
   * “Events” are inherently temporal; numbers are not.
   * Connect to analytic idealism (dashboard of experience).

## **5. Neurons and the Arrow of Time**

8. Neurons as the interface between the two times

   * A neuron's function cannot be run backwards (from output to input), so it can only be understood in terms of irreversible time
	** Its components include sodium/potassium pumps that are also irreversible:
		The components of sodium/potassium pumps can be described using pure, 		reversibleTime physics (ions, proteins etc.) but they have a higher level structure that embodies an irreversible function.
		The pump's function is to create an
		electro-chemical gradient (the action potential) that is converted to an impulse
		when the neuron fires.
		Notes:
				1. the pumps, like all functions, require energy to run.
				2. the pumps appear (to us) to be running continuously.
				3. energy is required to transition from reversible to irreversible time.

   * Energy → rest state → release of temporal impulses.
   * Neurons *generate* the arrow of time.
   * The use-mention idea, familiar from programming, resolves the paradox of neuron observation.

9. Measurement

   * Physics depends on measurements; neurons register these acts.
   * Therefore perceived time is foundational to physics’ use of time and space.

## **6. The Two Cultures: Arts & Sciences**

10. Why unification is needed

    * Arts: subjective/perceived time.
    * Sciences: physical/reversible time.
    * Art as expression of insight; not commerce.
    * Science uses proof/replication; art does not.

11. A New Kind of Art

    * Complement to Wolfram’s “A New Kind of Science”.
    * Must transcend commerce and institutional systems.

## **7. Programming as the Bridge**

12. Programming as a new kind of writing

    * Encapsulation, type hierarchies, use/mention distinction.
    * Multiple levels of meaning.
    * GitHub as “ratcheting” collaborative improvement.
    * Conventional apps vs evolving programs.

## **8. Free Will**

13. Free will and computational irreducibility

    * Large interacting neurons → unpredictability.
    * No system can fully know causes of its own decisions.
    * Still socially necessary to treat people as responsible.

## **9. Synchronicity & Perception**

14. Synchrony in physics vs synchrony in brains

    * Relativity’s spacetime vs subjective simultaneity.
    * 1ms threshold of perceived simultaneity.
    * 86 billion neurons ticking asynchronously.
    * Perceived time = high-level brain state changes.

15. Brain as a function

    * (1) sensory inputs
    * (2) internal state
    * (3) output events on an irreversible timeline.

16. “Space and time reduce complexity”

    * Long-held idea refined by the two-time model.

## **10. Consciousness**

17. Consciousness depends on perceived time

    * No time/space during unconsciousness.
    * Mistake: measuring subjective time by physical units.
    * Subjective present is richer than a physical instant.
    * Brain cannot model the whole universe → blind spot.

## **11. Flux and Evolution**

18. Science, mathematics, and brains evolve

    * Heraclitean flux again.
    * Evolution of neurons allows universe to observe itself.

## **12. Conclusions**

19. What consciousness is

    * Space/time as strategies for reducing complexity.
    * Neurons create subjective time out of energy.
    * Physical time depends on perceived time.
    * “Beginning of time” becomes meaningless.
    * Universe indifferent to human survival.

20. Stockhausen and subjective vs physical time

    * Performance practice and consciousness.

    * Early notation as chunking of events.
    * Tempo: subjective vs physical.
    * Dance, effort, intention.
