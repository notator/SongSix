1. Programming is A New Kind of Writing. Maybe that's a good working title, to follow my In Defence of Literacy. and Wolfram's A New Kind of Science. Wolfram's ideas are going to play a major role but, as I said, I don't think he understands what Art is (Its another way of describing the world). 2. Defining two types of time allows all of physics to remain valid within its domain of application (using reversible time), while allowing perceived time to operate properly in its own sphere (the Arts).
3. Programming languages now encapsulate some basic concepts from philosophy: type hierarchies, the use/mention distinction and,Āmore generally, the idea that there can be hierarchies of encapsulated information... Different, independent levels of meaning.
4. Describing how neurons work involves the use/mention distinction. I'm using my own neurons to describe (i.e. mention) neurons. Its a strange loop, but it agrees with Wolfram: computation is fundamental.
5. Its interesting that neurons seem to us to have evolved. So the universe must have (or have evolved) an intrinsic arrow of time that allows it (us, as part of the universe) to observe itself. Neurons are the physical embodiment of a function that takes an input, and uses/defines time to create an output. The actualĀidea of a neuron is outside time.
6. I think Heraclitus' Theory of Everything was right: "Everything is Flux". Its pointless trying to pinpoint a time when time doesn't exist. I think a version of his theory can be explained very precisely, using modern ideas! Note that science and mathematics also evolve!Ā But some things remain basically the same. :-)
7. I've been looking at the way neurons work. Its interesting that they use energy to maintain their rest state. (The rest state is a static imbalance of electrochemical potential that is waiting to be released as a temporal impulse.) So the universe is essentially using neurons to create time out of energy! :-) At least that's the way it looks to us! :-) Neurons are actually made of physical stuff that can be described using (reversible time) physics. But their overall physical structure and function have to be described at a higher level
