**Prompt4** What does Wolfram say about *irreversible* CAs?<br>
**Short answer:** Wolfram says that most cellular automata are *irreversible*, meaning you cannot uniquely reconstruct their past. He emphasizes that irreversibility is what allows them to generate randomness, complexity, and apparent entropy increase—paralleling how irreversible macroscopic phenomena emerge from reversible microscopic physics.  

---

### 🔄 Wolfram’s Key Points on Irreversible Cellular Automata
- **Most rules are irreversible**: In his studies, Wolfram found that the majority of simple rules (like the famous Rule 30 or Rule 110) are not reversible. Multiple different past states can lead to the same present, so backward evolution is ambiguous.  
- **Origins of randomness**: Wolfram argued that irreversibility is central to how cellular automata generate randomness. Even simple deterministic rules can produce unpredictable, seemingly random behavior because information about the past is lost.  
- **Entropy and thermodynamics**: He connected irreversibility in cellular automata to the Second Law of Thermodynamics. Just as entropy increases in physical systems, irreversible automata lose information about their past, creating apparent disorder.  
- **Universality and complexity**: Irreversible rules can still be computationally universal. Rule 110, for example, is irreversible but capable of universal computation, showing that irreversibility does not prevent complex or structured behavior.  
- **Contrast with reversible rules**: Wolfram highlighted that while reversible automata conserve information (like microscopic physics), irreversible automata better capture macroscopic phenomena such as diffusion, turbulence, and randomness.

| **Property** | **Reversible CA** | **Irreversible CA** |

| **Past reconstruction** | Unique predecessor | Multiple possible predecessors |

| **Behavior** | Symmetric, predictable | Random, chaotic, complex |
| **Examples** | Specially designed second-order rules | Rule 30, Rule 110 |

Sources: Wolfram’s *Cellular Automata and Complexity: Collected Papers*, *A New Kind of Science*.

### 🚀 Why It Matters
- **Physics connection**: Irreversible automata model how macroscopic irreversibility (like entropy increase) can emerge from simple rules.  
- **Computation**: Even irreversible rules can be universal, showing that loss of information doesn’t prevent computation.  
- **Complexity theory**: Irreversibility is a key ingredient in generating the richness and unpredictability Wolfram associates with “a new kind of science.”
