**Quick answer:** Stephen Wolfram studied special *reversible cellular automata* where every state has a unique predecessor. These include second-order rules and multi-state systems designed to mimic physical laws. Reversibility matters because it parallels how information is conserved in physics (like classical mechanics) and underpins reversible computing, which could reduce energy costs.

### 🔄 Examples of Reversible Rules Wolfram Explored
- **[Second-order cellular automata](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Second-order%20cellular%20automata)**: Wolfram showed that by defining rules based on both the current and previous states, one can construct automata that are reversible. These are explicitly designed so that the past can be reconstructed uniquely.  
- **[Multi-state reversible automata](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Multi-state%20reversible%20automata)**: For example, a one-dimensional automaton with nine states where each cell copies shape from its left neighbor and color from its right neighbor. This guarantees reversibility.  
- **[Reversible function repository rules](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Reversible%20function%20repository%20rules)**: Wolfram’s Function Repository includes reversible automata defined for two colors and range 1, where evolution can be run forward or backward.  
- **[Symmetry-based rules](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Symmetry-based%20rules)**: Some rules are reversible because their update functions are symmetric under time reversal, meaning the same logic applies forward and backward.

### ⚛️ Why Reversibility Matters in Physics
- **[Information conservation](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Information%20conservation)**: In classical physics, the laws of motion are reversible—knowing the present state lets you reconstruct the past. Reversible automata mirror this property.  
- **[Second Law of Thermodynamics](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Second%20Law%20of%20Thermodynamics)**: Wolfram discusses how irreversibility in physics (entropy increase) emerges from reversible microscopic rules, just as irreversible automata can be built from reversible foundations.  
- **[Modeling physical systems](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Modeling%20physical%20systems)**: Reversible automata are used to simulate gases, fluids, and other systems where microscopic reversibility leads to macroscopic statistical behavior.  

---

### 💻 Why Reversibility Matters in Computation
- **[Reversible computing](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Reversible%20computing)**: In theory, computation can be done without losing information. This is important because information loss is tied to energy dissipation (Landauer’s principle).  
- **[Efficiency](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Efficiency)**: Reversible automata provide a model for designing computers that minimize heat generation, potentially enabling ultra-efficient computation.  
- **[Cryptography and complexity](guide://action?prefill=Tell%20me%20more%20about%3A%20Cryptography%20and%20complexity)**: Wolfram explored reversible automata for secure sequence generation and complexity theory, since reversibility ensures no information is destroyed.
